My Story

I am a current graduate student at the School of Computing & Information Sciences at Willamette University, pursuing a master's degree in data science. I earned my B.S. in Data Science with a minor in Biology in 2024, and I'm continuing to apply the computational techniques I learn to insect taxonomy, my primary area of passion and interest. Since 2022, I have conducted research on Oregon's native bees in Briana Lindh's bee lab. By blending traditional methods of biological surveys and analysis with new technologies, I hope to streamline future research efforts for our lab. I am currently applying computer vision, bioinformatics, database engineering, and species distribution mapping to the data we've gathered over the years, driven by the goal of improving our understanding of Oregon's vital, yet easily overlooked pollinator ecologies.
